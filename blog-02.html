Small incremental improvements every day lead to massive change on the long term.

How a small mishap improved my money management

We set aside a strict budget for each month. It's the same for every month. The rest, I transfer to the savings account at the start of the month.

This time, I made a mistake, and transferred to soon, which led me to pay the electricity bill from the savins account. I realised, for a multitude of reasons. This is actually way simpler.

I decided to continue doing it this way from now on. A mistake turns into an improvement.

It reminded me of this idea from the world famous book "Thinking fast and slow".

If 25 department managers are asked to consider a risky option in which revenues are either doubled or halved, they will not take the bet. However, if the CEO of these 25 managers is asked the same question, he would want them all to take the bets. Statistical aggregation will mitigate the overall risk.

The way we are build today is because of mistakes in evolution.

We humans can do a lot if things intentionally. We have the capability to plan and think ahead, and not to rely on mistakes to evolve.

But we can't plan it all. Leaving space for mistakes, leaves space for evolution and progress!
